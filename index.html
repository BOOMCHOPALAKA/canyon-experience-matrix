Canyon Experience Matrix

A canyon's rating should include getting in and out, not just the rope work. So this scores Approach and Exit for general effort, rolls them into Access, and blends it with technical Difficulty into one tunable Adventure Score. And for good measure there's a wasp exposure read too — an underrated data point if you'd rather not spend your day getting stung, especially if you're allergic. Click a header to sort; drag the sliders to reweight.

All canyon data sourced from RopeWiki, the community canyoneering wiki.

The axes (all 1–5)

Difficulty — ACA grade (always counts)
Access — effort in/out (always counts)
Wasp — seasonal, Jul–Oct only
Adventure = Diff + Access, +wasp in season

Rating scale · 1 → 5

1–2 easy / beginner
3 moderate / intermediate
4 hard / advanced
5 arduous / expert

Adventure Score weights

The score blends Difficulty and Access (a canyon's always-true character). Weight them however you like — some prioritize the technical grade, others care most about an easy hike in and out. Wasp exposure is seasonal, so it only folds in when you flip the toggle for a summer/fall trip.

👆 New here? Hover the ⓘ on any column to see what that rating means and how it's calculated. Click any column header to sort by it (click again to flip highest/lowest-first). And click a canyon name to open its full RopeWiki page.
